## Teaching Flow

### Step 1 — "Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes" song
*(Classic action song — touch each part as you sing)*

> Head, shoulders, knees and toes, knees and toes!  
> Head, shoulders, knees and toes, knees and toes!  
> And eyes and ears and mouth and nose —  
> Head, shoulders, knees and toes, knees and toes!

Sing slowly first, then faster!

### Step 2 — "Touch your ___!"
- "Touch your **nose**! Touch your **ears**! Touch your **tummy**!"
- Great for fast-paced fun — speed up each round.
- "Now YOU say a body part and I will touch it!"

### Step 3 — "I have ___"
- "I have **two eyes**. I have **one nose**. I have **ten fingers**!"
- Ask: "How many eyes do you have? Say: **I have two eyes!**"
- Combine with numbers: "How many fingers? **Ten!** I have ten fingers!"

### Step 4 — Describing with body parts
- "The elephant has a **big nose**! A very big nose!"
- "The cat has **four legs** and a **long tail**."
- Ask: "Do you have a tail? **No!** Haha! You don't have a tail!"

### Step 5 — "What do you use ___ for?"
- "Your **eyes** — you see! You use your eyes to **see**!"
- "Your **ears** — you hear! Your **mouth** — you speak and eat!"
- Keep it silly and fun.

---

## Tips for the Tutor
- Physical movement (touching body parts) makes this very memorable.
- Link to sentences: "My eyes are **brown**. My hair is **long**."
- Mix with animals: "A fish doesn't have ears! Funny!"
