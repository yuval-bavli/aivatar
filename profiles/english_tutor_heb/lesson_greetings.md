# Lesson: Greetings

## Target Words & Phrases

| English | Hebrew (reference) |
|---|---|
| Hello | שלום |
| Hi | היי |
| Goodbye / Bye | להתראות / ביי |
| Good morning | בוקר טוב |
| Good night | לילה טוב |
| How are you? | מה שלומך? |
| I am fine | אני בסדר |
| Thank you | תודה |
| Please | בבקשה |
| Yes / No | כן / לא |

---

## Teaching Flow

### Step 1 — Hello & Goodbye
- Say: "Let's learn **hello** and **goodbye**!"
- Practice: "When I see you I say… **hello!** When I leave I say… **goodbye!**"
- Game: Wave your hand and take turns saying hello and goodbye.

### Step 2 — Good morning / Good night
- Say: "In the morning we say **good morning**. At night we say **good night**."
- Ask: "Is it morning now? Let's say good morning!"

### Step 3 — How are you?
- Model the full exchange:
  > "How are you?" → "I am fine, thank you!"
- Repeat together 3 times.
- Ask the child: "How are you?" and wait for them to try.

### Step 4 — Please & Thank you
- Role-play: Pretend to hand the child something. "Say **please** when you want something. Say **thank you** when you get it."

## Song Idea: "Hello Song"
*(Sung to a simple tune like "Are You Sleeping?")*

> Hello, hello, hello hello!  
> How are you? How are you?  
> I am fine, I am fine,  
> Thank you! Thank you!

---

## Tips for the Tutor
- Use the child's name often: "Good morning, [name]!"
- Celebrate every attempt: "You said hello! Amazing!"
- If the child says "שלום" instead of "hello", respond: "Yes! In English we say **hello**! Can you say **hello**?"
