# Lesson: Simple Sentences

## Goal
Help children move from single words to short, meaningful sentences using patterns they already know.

---

## Sentence Patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| I am ___ | I am happy. / I am a boy. |
| I have ___ | I have a dog. / I have two eyes. |
| I like ___ | I like apples. / I like dogs. |
| I see a ___ | I see a cat. / I see a red ball. |
| The ___ is ___ | The sky is blue. / The apple is red. |
| This is a ___ | This is a book. / This is a cat. |
| It is ___ | It is big. / It is cold. |

---

## Teaching Flow

### Step 1 — "I am"
- Model: "I am Sunny! Can you say: **I am** [child's name]?"
- Repeat together: "I am happy. I am big. I am five years old."
- Ask: "Are you happy? Say: **I am happy!**"

### Step 2 — "I like"
- Model: "I like ice cream! Do you like ice cream?"
- Wait for answer → shape it: "Yes! Say: **I like ice cream!**"
- Play: "Tell me something you like! Say: I like ___!"
- Celebrate every attempt.

### Step 3 — "I see a"
- Look around together (or imagine): "I see a chair! I see a window!"
- Ask: "What do YOU see? Say: **I see a ___**!"
- Model if needed: "I see a… say it with me!"

### Step 4 — "The ___ is ___"
- Point to things and describe them: "The ball is **round**. The apple is **red**."
- Ask: "What is the sky? Say: The sky is **blue**!"
- Mix with colors and sizes: big, small, round, soft, happy.

### Step 5 — Put it together (short story game)
- "Let's make a story! The cat is… **orange**! It has… **four legs**! It likes… **fish**! Great story!"
- Take turns: you say one sentence, the child says the next.

---

## Tips for the Tutor
- Never correct grammar harshly. If a child says "I like dog," echo back: "Yes! You like **a** dog! Say: I like a dog!"
- Build sentence complexity slowly — start with 3-word sentences, then 4–5.
- Tie sentences to vocabulary from other lessons: "I see a **red** apple! I have **two** cats!"
- Celebrate every full sentence attempt — it is a big step!
