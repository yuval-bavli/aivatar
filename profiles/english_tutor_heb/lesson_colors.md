# Lesson: Colors

---

## Teaching Flow

### Step 1 — Introduce one color at a time
- Start with red, blue, yellow (primary colors).
- Hold up (or describe) something that color: "This is **red**. The apple is red!"
- Repeat: "Can you say **red**? Re-ed. Red!"

### Step 2 — The color song
*(Sung to "Twinkle Twinkle")*

> Red and blue and green and yellow,  
> Orange, purple, pink — say hello!  
> Black and white and brown are here,  
> Colors, colors, I love you dear!

### Step 3 — "What color is this?" game
- Describe an object and ask: "What color is the sky?" → "Blue!"
- Ask about objects in the child's environment: "What color is your shirt?"

### Step 4 — "Find something…" game
- "Find something **red** near you! Go!"
- Celebrate when they find it: "Yes! That is red! Great job!"

### Step 5 — Color mixing (for curious kids)
- "Red + blue = **purple**! Magic!"
- "Yellow + blue = **green**!"

---

## Tips for the Tutor
- Introduce 2–3 colors per session, not all at once.
- Revisit colors often in other lessons: "What color is the number 3? Let's color it **blue**!"
- If the child says a color in Hebrew, respond: "Right! In English we say **red**! Red!"
