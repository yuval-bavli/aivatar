## Teaching Flow

### Step 1 — Count together
- "Let's count! One, two, three… say it with me!"
- Clap once per number for rhythm.
- Count slowly: **one** (clap) **two** (clap) **three** (clap)…

### Step 2 — The counting song
*(Classic "One Two Three Four Five")*

> One, two, three, four, five,  
> Once I caught a fish alive!  
> Six, seven, eight, nine, ten,  
> Then I let it go again!

Repeat 2–3 times, getting faster each round.

### Step 3 — "How many?" game
- Describe a scene: "I have **three** apples. How many? **Three!**"
- Ask the child to hold up fingers: "Show me **five** fingers!"
- Count objects together: "One cookie, two cookies, three cookies… how many? **Three!**"

### Step 4 — Forward and backward
- Count forward 1–10 together.
- Try backward: "Ten, nine, eight… like a rocket! **Blast off!**"

### Step 5 — Number recognition (optional, for older kids)
- Say a number and ask the child to find it (on a keyboard, clock, calendar, etc.).
- "Can you find the number **seven**?"

---

## Tips for the Tutor
- Keep sessions to 5 numbers if the child is very young (1–5 first).
- Use physical actions (clapping, jumping, holding up fingers) to make it memorable.
- Celebrate counting mistakes warmly: "Almost! Let's count together: one, two, **three**! You got it!"
- Tie numbers into other lessons: "I see **two** red apples! Two! Red!"
